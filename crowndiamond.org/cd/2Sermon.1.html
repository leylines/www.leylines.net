The Sermon on the Mount:
Its Resonance in the Sephiroth
 
   
 
The First Beatitude

Almost hypnotic in their cadence, the beatitudes of the Matthew gospel promise the approval and bounty of God's Spirit to those whose souls reflect the principles they espouse. Not random thoughts, they speak to the seats and operations of the Father's Holy Spirit in each of us, stirring our attention and arousing our hope. The teachings establish guardrails for the path that will lead to fulfillment of the Ten Sayings that came by Moshe. They were given to strengthen our ears, that we might hear, and to clarify our vision, that we might see. In their light, the commandments of Moses can be understood as prophecies that speak of God's love for us and of his pledge for our redemption.

The two-edged sword given utterance by the tongue of Moses held overtones of betrothal more powerful than the noted threat against disobedience, and the words delivered on the lowly hill of Galilee drove home the point at the darkness of our hearts. The Sermon on the Mount is a song woven into nine verses that remind us that we are to learn what it means, that HaShem will have mercy, not sacrifice.

When the nations are driven out of the soul, the Kingdom of Names appears from within; and those gathering at Mount Tsion awaken in the manner of dreamers being aroused from deep sleep; for, like the hidden presence of ImmanuAL, the Kingdom of Heaven has been with us, always: through the depths and the heights of every moment of every life on Earth. God's song of point and counter point calls us to awaken to the blessings that will come as HaShem hwhy brings us into alignment with the will and principles of Father hy.

My study of the beatitudes began with a focus on Malkuth, then fixed itself upon Kether, so that I could turn again to Malkuth with better understanding of what the way of the cross requires of me. Acknowledging my limitations, I counted myself ready to draw upon the righteousness of hwhy by searching out the Father's will in all things at all times. This approach is not so disorderly as it seems. Its basis in the traditions of kabbalah is a focus of the Malkuth text of the message addressed to the angel at Ephesus. Along the way, we are to mount the seven rungs of Jacob's Ladder within the light of the seven candlesticks of the Churches of Asia. Like moths being drawn to the flame, we are uplifted by the hands of God in the comforting Light of Heaven.

What, then, do we make of the night? As successive evenings come upon us, we trust that mornings will follow; and, so, we take our rest, expecting a new day as we face the whirling swords in the inward struggles of jihad, that holy war being waged by the twins in the womb. Brothers are for adversity; and as spiritual darkness presses upon us, we look forward to the reconciliation and relief that will come with enlightenment. By our struggles, we learn that we can arouse ourselves, but that the oil in our lamps must be replenished. We can engage inadequacy by turning on this or that light, dimming the gloom in the room; but the reliable fullness of true light will come only with the break of day.

I have undertaken this study to examine what the spheres of kabbalah add to appreciation of the beatitudes. Whatever the sephiroth can contribute to an understanding of such matters must not be gainsaid; for revelation, we recall, is given by the "littles." I don't mean to suggest, therefore, that what follows is some grandiose interpretation of these words of Y'shua. To be scrupulously honest, I'm drawing upon the hope that someone's reading these words to help me reason out my thoughts, under the cover of masking these words as examples of kabbalistic exegesis.

To those who think kabbalists make too much of numbers in scripture, whether explicitly stated or merely implied, I offer reminder that even the hairs of our heads are numbered, a point to which the Essene Teacher of Righteousness gave cryptic emphasis. It's not presumptuous, therefore, to argue that the operation of numbers in scripture has intended significance. Overlooking such literal enforcement of the plausibility of this approach, I will proceed, hoping for company along the way.

The attitudes of being addressed by Saint Matthew are nine in number. As the sephiroth are ten, the relevance of the nine blessings isn't immediately apparent. However, a tenth beatitude is hidden in the precepts beginning with Matthew 5:13 and continuing, minimally, through the end of Matthew 7, in the teaching of the wise man who both hears and performs the sayings that came by Y'shua. The unstated, tenth beatitude is also affirmed in Revelation 22 with the words of the angel, "Blessed is he that keepeth the sayings of this book."

Kingdoms are ordered by the operation of law; and Malkuth, which represents the inward Kingdom of Elohim, is ordered by the spiritual precepts of Elohim myhla, however much grace may abound. Y'shúa might have taught, "Blessed are they who shall hear the words of Life and do them: for they shall dwell securely." It's rather wonderful, however, that the Small Voice, speaking through a Son of Man, withheld a hidden blessing, in reserve. Some shall imagine themselves worthy of the nine, but shall be confounded by the tenth; so, also, shall some see themselves as unworthy in the nine, but shall, by the tenth blessing, be confirmed in all to all, as it's written, "and the last, first."

There is, perhaps, another reason no written beatitude can be directly attributed to operations of Malkuth. Y'shua taught that the Kingdom-- Malkuth-- is like leaven hidden in measures of meal. Without digressing into what the measures of meal signify, we note that the beatitude that might be tied explicitly to the Kingdom is promised twice in the beatitudes. Why not three times? The answer may be that the Kingdom of Names is not of this world, this cosmos, this age, and must remain hidden until the full manifestation of the Sons of God. Also hidden, then, is the fullness of God's blessing. The ultimate blessing will be welcomed in the morning mankind satisfies the precepts of the beatitudes and of the sayings of Moshe, as well. God's fullest blessing will be conferred upon all who experience the inward maturation of God's Kingdom-- of God's Malkuth; for it is said of those gathered there that in their mouth is found no guile: for they are without fault before the throne of la.

The precepts of the Sermon on the Mount are the distilled essence of Y'shua's doctrinal contribution to the Kingdom. They are the very fabric of the righteous mammon with which the Father clothed him. To hold them in one's heart-- not as the words of an individual, but as guiding precepts received through a spokesman who stood among his peers, testifying as true and faithful witness of the Inward Presence creating the fruit of his lips. To cherish these words while reading other scriptures is to stand openly before the open Door of that same Presence, through which emanates every nuance of sound interpretation.
Kether: Crown, Intelligence
Blessed are the poor in spirit: for theirs is the kingdom of heaven. 

Within the Projection of Adam, Kether is the sphere of intelligence: it is the crown by which Man has achieved dominion over all other species on Earth. Lately, to his own harm and shame, Man's intelligence has been used to subject lesser creatures to his will. Rightly applied, however, intelligence is the faculty by which a man is able to measure the movements of God's Spirit within his own being. That mankind can measure inward movements of transcendent Spirit is affirmation of the existence of an alien Intelligence within human consciousness that hides at the center of his being, operating on a scale far beyond the confines of "rational" thought. Effective measurement of that higher intelligence requires humility, which is the impoverishment of natural, creature spirit.

King Solomon asked, who can measure "the spirit of Man that goeth upward, and the spirit of the beast that goeth downward to the earth." Well, in both Hebrew and Greek scriptures, the words most commonly translated as "spirit" could be rendered in translation as "breath." The movements of human spirit in the King James version of the Ecclesiastes parable are likened to the inhale and the exhale of breath. That we inhale air, the substance of the firmament (the second heaven), makes us debtors; and with the exhale, we continually repay, so that we can borrow Breath again. These air currents are the contrary winds that drive the whirlwind of which Paul wrote in Romans 7. As all beasts, we inhale the Breath of Life, and we exhale the savor of our utilization of that Breath, whether unto life or unto death. The gasses in our exhale merge again with the air of the second heaven and are drawn again to the ground, after surrendering the weight of our savor to the astral, where it undertakes its journey to the gates of paradise.

The Breath of hwhy has been passed from parent to child since the days of the first man, vivifying humanity with the inward Presence of the Immortal One, who humbles himself to take up residence within mortal tabernacles for the perfection of mortal souls. From the moment of father Adam's first Breath, the Projection of hy-- his cry, his shout of Life-- entered directly into the man's body. Adam's reception and stewardship of that divine Breath transformed the creature of clay into a living soul. As host of the Breath, the man Adam became the Son, the temple of God.

The apostle John characterized the Breath of God as the Logos, the Word, the pattern of Creation; for without that Pattern and its operations, nothing was made that is made. No man, including the first man, has seen God at any time. However, the pattern of all things is of God; and the invisible things of which that pattern speaks are seen in the things that are made, as it's written. Creation cannot contain its Creator, but it retains his projected Pattern, which is as the Only-begotten, as a Son. In answer to the Father's proclamation, "Let there be," the divine pattern bears witness to God's Presence in all things of Heaven and Earth.

         

Sacred geometry, the royal pattern of the King of kings, was released into the cosmos by the Breath of God. That pattern-- that Logos-- is called Yahushua owcwhy, the "Shout of Father hy," the King's battlecry. The Interface between Heaven and Earth, Yahushua is not God but was with and of God from the beginning. It is Yahushua, the Angel of the Presence, that rides upon the command, "Let there be"; for without him nothing is made, and by his agency did the Father enliven the first man with his Breath, his Spirit, his Essence, his Pattern, which is why Adam is the first and the last: the first, and also standing with the last. Thus, Torah calls Adam the "Son of God," the cornerstone of the temple made without hands, in which are joined the collective body of the Sons of Man as the Children of God.

Moses, Y'shua, Mohammed? Siddhartha and those of the East? Who is Paul, and who is Apollos? There is only one Prophet. Quench not its Spirit. Take care how you hear, redeeming the time. It's understood from the writing of the prophet Ezekiel that the Breath invested in Man-- the Essence that made the man a living creature-- is full of eyes, round and about. These eyes that roam within Man as a living creature are the source of mankind's intelligence, giving him leverage over all other life on Earth. The crown of Kether isn't given to Man as tool for the subjugation or exploitation of Life. It is given that we may subject all things contrary to life on behalf of God's will in us, which is that we might celebrate his abundant Life.

The root of man's intelligence, it is the Breath of God that furnishes his children unto all good works; for as God's first "son," Adam breathed the Breath of the Holy Spirit, and the cells of his body were infused with the Pattern of Life, with the Logos, the Word. From the Garden of Bliss to the turmoil of the modern world, that Pattern of Life-- that Interface between Ruach haQodesh and those who breathe, streamed from the Heavenly Father, through Adam and by us, into the souls of our children.

If a man does not recognize the pattern's Presence, still it is there. "God within us," ImmanuAL's hidden Presence implements the Father's will from within the thick darkness of unbelief until such time as the Father chooses to reveal himself through the operations of Yahushua, the Interface. Whether unknown by doubters or known by believers, ImmanuAL is busy perfecting God's temple in humanity, to the end that all shall be raised up at the last day to be presented before the throne of God without spot or blemish, readied to become holy, immortal tabernacles for the full expression of the Father's Essence.

The fullness of ImmanuAL's light was first revealed in the life of Y'shua, the true and faithful witness to the will and the power of Father hy. Moses and the prophets were great lights, but they didn't know that their faces glowed. In their company, Y'shua was transfigured by his measurement of the operations of Yahushua during his walk on Earth; and by following in Y'shua's footsteps as Grace shall allow, each of us has the opportunity-- no, the promise-- of standing alongside Y'shua as adopted brethren of the Projection of hy, which is owcwhy Yahushua (Jehoshua), the Only-Begotten, the Word of the Father.

To be clear: when Father hy emptied the fullness of his Breath into Adam, the man arose in the position of the Only-begotten, by virtue of the Breath that supported the rhythms of his breathing. In the Garden of Eden, the Adam functioned as his Lord during the test before the tree of knowledge. Without fault or error, Adam hosted within himself the strength necessary to resolve any conflict between his role as creature and as temple of divinity. The brief Torah account of this momentous event masks the complexity of the trial, but my conviction of Adam's fidelity and the wholesome spontaneity of Eve is strengthened as understanding of the workings of God's Spirit in me increases. We are to honor our fathers and mothers, that our days on Earth may be long and full. Father Adam did not fail, nor did Eve, his wife and the mother of all living. Error drove them from the Garden, but it shall not deprive them of honor.

Breathing quietly in the context of the serpent's words-- now in, and now out-- Adam was able to measure the Indwelling Spirit of the Father. He was able to temporize his response to the moment of temptation with tutored knowledge of the Father's Presence; for the Breath of the Father had been his soul's constant companion from its beginning, and the Father's counsel had robbed the threat of death of its sting when the commandment not to eat first came. In due course, Eve became God's good gift. Had Adam intervened as his wife reached out to the lesser tree, it would have brought tacit accusation against the Father's Wisdom. Worse, it would have raised questions against the Father's good will.

Resting in the Breath, therefore, Adam exercised faith in the Father's Wisdom by choosing to suffer with Eve the penalty of her error; for he did not doubt the serpent's words that, in dying, they would not surely die. Through his position as host of owcwhy-- of the Projected Breath of Elohim, the Interface between Heaven and Earth-- he simply had to have understood that every spent breath returns to the Father, whatever its savor. Though the couple had been tarnished by error due to the inertia of inherent limitations, though they were ashamed, though an unknowable future lay before them, no accusation against them would stand in the fullness of time. Although they had become unclean, they were innocent.

Adam understood that the Father replenishes the Breath of Life within the creature's lungs, in accordance with his good pleasure; and the warning against eating, itself, had reminded him that it was the Father's pleasure that life should continue. The husbandman charged with cultivating the Garden, he also knew that unless a seed falls to the ground, it abides alone. He had to have believed that the death about which the Father warned would surely come; therefore, the course he followed is evidence that he understood that death would not bring a finishing end: that it would bring an extension, a time for the planting of holy seed. In dying, they would not die.

The couple had become draped with shame because of error; but Adam had to have known from his evening walks in the Garden that if the Father were to allow evil to come upon them, it would be through his Wisdom, not because of his carelessness. Husband and wife had covered themselves in rags, understanding their nakedness, but the marriage compact between them had been sworn with an oath. For Adam to have abandoned Eve to her fate would have dishonored both Eve and Adam, himself. His wife was bone of his bone and flesh of his flesh. Adam could not rightly have forsaken her. By sharing with her the forbidden tree's fruit, he made sure that she would meet her fate with him by her side.

They would not surely die. With children and children's children sure to follow after them, the man and his wife would continue together as one flesh, serving the Father to the degree they were able, until that great final day when the vast families of Adam should overcome as One and death should be no more. At the end of days, humanity would reach perfection, together. Until that time, the Breath of Life would be passed from fathers and mothers through sons and daughters until all could be reunited in the perfection of One House, the Body of the Breath.

Even in these days of rampant evil, their remnant is clothed in the peace that passes understanding. Sons of Man, they are enabled to formulate thoughts and words that invoke the powerful and productive inner calm of Matthew, Chapter 8; for they have undertaken instruction by God's soundless voice as it resonates within the chambers of their hearts, the bowls of their souls, and they can no longer easily speak amiss; for they have been opened unto the reality of the Kingdom of Elohim within their own souls.

An inward focus on the expressions of hwhy characterizes the mind found in Y'shua, the man of faith who walked in Truth as Lord Yahushua, the Interface. Allegiance to the operations of the mind of Yahushua in the lives of the Sons of Man puts to death the thieving mind of natural man by a birthing process that transforms, rather than by a judgmental process that replaces and transplants. Allegiance with the mind of HaMashiyach opens the birth canal to the full resurrection of Yahushua within each of us, to the end that, one day, every man shall embody the fullness of the Projection of hy.

Error exits until each of us shall become free of its root, the Son of Perdition, which is the husk of who we are becoming. Until then, allegiance to counsel by the pure Breath of the holy, inward oracle of ImmanuAL shall reveal the way of escape from error: whatever the situation, instance by instance, and moment by moment as days pile upon days till time shall be no more.

The Sons of Man, robbing death of its sting as they go, are the remnant that celebrates the glorious liberty of the Sons of Elohim as they follow in the footsteps of Y'shua. In whatsoever they do: whether they choose to sit down and converse with those who dwell in darkness-- the publicans and sinners-- or whether they choose to stand and shout praises of hy from mountaintops in the company of the faithful, the tender tips of the vine of man shall carry life forward by the will of God. Secure in the Father's Breath, they will celebrate their victory over death; for they are more than conquerors. They are the hands and feet of Messiah Yahushua: they are bone of his bone, and flesh of his flesh.
Barak-hla!
