The Temple Within
 
The body is the temple of God, whose light shines within its thick darkness and the darkness neither comprehends it nor is it capable of withstanding the energies that come with the Presence that enlightens all men who come into the world: good and bad. In each of us, messiah has his throne, and we are to bow before it: not cower, but bow, in acknowledgement of the grandeur, the unspeakable blessing of the Breath of Life first entrusted to Adam and, these many thousands of years later, now entrusted to each of us.

The illustration portrays the organization of a practical kabbalah. No knowledge is required to practice worship in this manner. God is in my loins: I send my spirit there to measure the energy, the presence of the Life that is there, knowing that Mashiyach is the Way, the Truth, and the Life, all of which contribute to the light within the inward house of God.

I trace that energy from my loins to my stomach. I measure the energy. I monitor the thoughts associated with this seat. I allow the energy to build. And so it is with each of the energy fields, as seated within the chakras of the body. I walk in this energy, and therefore I walk in HaMashiyach, who speaks through the angels at each chakra, saying this is the way you should go: walk in it, giving no thought to what you should say or do, but accepting those things in the hour they shall be required.
