Ezekiel's Vision

When I began to extend the lines of Adam Kadmon beyond its perimeter, I recalled Ezekiel's chariot vision. I though of the reaching lines as wings extending from the paths of the Tree of Life, as echoed in the words of Isaiah 6:2: "Above it stood the seraphim; each one had six wings; with two he covered his face, and with two he covered his feet, and with two he did fly:"

Isaiah's vision was situated in the heavens, whereas Ezekiel's vision was situated upon Earth; thus Ezekiel saw but four wings, two stretching upward and two covering the bodies.
